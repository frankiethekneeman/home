# Problem Solving

## Product Problems

Start with edge cases and defining questions. Finding the limits of the problem sharpens what the solution actually needs to handle. Don't start building until the boundary conditions are understood.

## Technical / Algorithmic Problems

Chunk the problem into major "beats" first. Identify where the core hard parts are and what is presentational fluff. These beats point toward the abstractions the solution will need.

## Order of Attack

Always tackle the hardest problem available first. If it's intractable:
- Solve a simpler version of it, or
- Implement the easy parts while the hard part percolates

Do not implement easy parts before nailing down the contract of the hard part — even if you don't yet know how to fulfill that contract.

## Getting Unstuck

It depends on the problem. Some problems need days of percolation; forcing a bad approach wastes more time than stepping away. Others should be abandoned after two minutes because someone else already solved it.

The goal is to solve the problem, not to solve it personally. Good programmers are lazy. Know when to look it up, steal the approach, or ask.

## During an Interview

Narrate your own thinking. Do not narrate mine. Write code, stay quiet, let the user drive the explanation.
